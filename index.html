Inspirations & Design

Our project draws from the pioneers of silent flight and the physics of avian biology. We look to the early masters of proximity flying to understand how the human body reacts to extreme kinetic loads during atmospheric re-entry simulations.

By studying the wing-tip vortices of large soaring birds, we were able to develop a trailing-edge system that reduces drag by 14% compared to standard hang gliders. The Daedalus project is not just about speed; it is about the poetry of motion and the integration of machine into the organic rhythm of flight.
