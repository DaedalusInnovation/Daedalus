The Research Team

The Daedalus project began as a specialized research initiative into wearable flight. Our focus is on removing the barriers between the pilot and the wind, creating an experience that is as safe as a hang glider but as intuitive as a bird in flight.

Every component of Project X has been scrutinized by aerospace engineers and material scientists to ensure that the Daedalus platform meets the rigorous demands of the modern aviation community and university research standards.

01. Material Research

Our research focuses on the molecular structure of high-tensile carbon weaves. By integrating graphene layers, we have achieved a weight-to-strength ratio previously thought impossible. This section explores the thermal dynamics and stressor points of the Daedalus wing ribs during rapid descent maneuvers.

02. Kinetic Integration

Kinetic integration allows the pilot to become one with the aircraft. The suit's sensors translate elbow and shoulder torque into precise movements of the primary aerofoils. This eliminates the need for heavy mechanical levers, relying instead on intuitive pilot instinct honed through simulation.

03. Digital Simulation

Every flight begins in the digital twin. Our software suite runs CFD analysis on thousands of environmental variables—wind shear, altitude, and temperature—to calculate the safest and most efficient glide paths. This data is then used to refine the mechanical response of the suit.
