THE NATURAL STANDARD

ENGINEERING GIANTS

Hang gliders, wingsuits, and parasails are amazing recreational achievements that bring us a step closer to true flight. Yet, they often rely on gravity or specific weather conditions, falling short in active control, speed, and total self-propulsion.

WINGS

One Wing. Every Function.

Morphing Architecture

We believe that with only one set of wings, a pilot should be able to experience easy takeoff, efficient cruising, fast dives, and quick turns. This versatility is made possible by physically morphing the wings in flight.

The suit is a one-piece design; the wings work in tandem with the body unit to provide a wearable, aerodynamic shell. To achieve this, the wing structure is broken into three main segments that fit the dimensional criteria of the human form while maintaining high-performance output.

Dimensional Properties & Wing Load

To effectively carry a human, we look to nature's most efficient glider: the Wandering Albatross. An albatross typically has a wing load of approx. 24 kg/m². Our engineering goal is to match this natural upper limit.

  • Max Pilot Weight 100 kg
  • Est. Craft Weight 40 kg
  • Total Mass 140 kg
  • Target Wing Load 25 kg/m²
  • Req. Surface Area 5.6 m²

Mechanical Segmentation

The morphing mechanism is symmetrical, but for this breakdown, we analyze one side. Each wing half is divided into two primary sections: a stationary base and a moving array.

The moving section consists of 3 pivoting "panels." Including the stationary base, there are 4 overlapping segments per wing. These segments are cut to precise geometries (Figure X) to ensure they morph under one another smoothly without mechanical binding.

Tension-Based Actuation

All 4 segments share a central joint. This pivot point creates a smooth transition during the morphing process.

The system utilizes heavy-duty springs to keep the wings in a "natural resting position" of fully extended. This is a safety feature; if power is lost, the wings automatically spread to maximum glide surface area.

TAIL

Stability & Flight Control

The Aerodynamic Glue

The Tail is the glue that holds the whole design together. Its surface area is specifically matched to the surface area of the wings to balance and control the center of lift for the system. This results in stable flight.

Pitch Authority

The Tail also is the center of flight controls. With the ability to move up/down the tail can change the angle of attack aka the pitch of the suit in order to generate lift or initiate a dive.

SYSTEMS & UI

The Digital Nervous System

Holographic HUD

Information is survival. Our helmet visor integrates a transparent OLED Heads-Up Display (HUD) that projects critical telemetry—altitude, airspeed, battery life, and artificial horizon—directly into the pilot's line of sight without obstructing the view of the world below.

Neural-Link Integration

To reduce reaction latency, the FALCON utilizes non-invasive EMG sensors lining the suit's interior. These sensors detect muscle activation milliseconds before movement occurs, priming the control surfaces for instant response.

  • Latency < 8ms
  • Sensor Density 40 nodes/limb
  • OS Architecture Real-Time Linux Kernel

SAFETY

Redundancy is Religion

Triple-Redundant Electronics

Every electronic signal is processed by three independent flight computers. If one fails, the others vote it out and maintain control. This aerospace-standard architecture ensures that a computer glitch never results in a loss of flight control.

Ballistic Recovery System

In the event of total structural failure or pilot unconsciousness, a pyrotechnic charge deploys a whole-system parachute. This parachute is capable of slowing the descent of the pilot and the equipment to a survivable velocity from as low as 100 feet.

STRUCTURE

Generative Design

AI-Optimized Topology

We do not design shapes; we grow them. Using generative AI algorithms, we simulate thousands of flight hours to remove every gram of material that isn't structurally necessary. The result is a skeletal frame that looks organic—bone-like—but possesses the strength of titanium.

Material Matrix

  • Primary Frame Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer
  • Joints Sintered Titanium 6Al-4V
  • Skin Ripstop Dyneema Composite
